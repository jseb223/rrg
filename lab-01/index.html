Where is the Red River Gorge?

The Red River Gorge is a nationally renowned park in central Kentucky. The gorge is located in Powell and Wolfe counties and also sits in the Daniel Boone National Forest.

"The complex topography of Red River Gorge provides habitat for a rich diversity of plants and animals. Visitors will find maples, oaks, tulip poplar, pine, ash, walnut and eastern hemlocks among the many species of trees that grow here. There are also many wildflowers to be found here as well, including Bloodroot, Hepatica, Trillium, Purple Phacelia, Crested Dwarf Iris, and Pink and Yellow Lady's Slippers, just to name a few. Some of the trails known for outstanding spring wildlfowers include Swift Camp Creek, Wildcat, Rock Bridge, Whittleton Branch and Whittleton Arch" www.hikinginthesmokys.com.
